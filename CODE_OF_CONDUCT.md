# Veltrix Code of Conduct

## Our Mission Context

Veltrix is a systems-level programming language and compiler ecosystem focused on correctness, clarity, and long-term maintainability.  
As infrastructure software, it requires discipline, professionalism, and respect.

This Code of Conduct defines the behavioral standards required to participate in the Veltrix community.

---

## Our Pledge

We — maintainers, contributors, and community members — pledge to foster a professional, respectful, and harassment-free environment for everyone, regardless of:

- Age  
- Disability  
- Ethnicity  
- Gender identity or expression  
- Level of experience  
- Nationality  
- Race  
- Religion  
- Sexual orientation  
- Socioeconomic status  

Participation in Veltrix is a privilege contingent upon maintaining these standards.

---

## Professional Standards

Because Veltrix is a compiler and language project, we maintain a high bar for discourse and collaboration.

### Expected Behavior

- Communicate with precision and technical clarity.
- Critique ideas, designs, and implementations — **not individuals**.
- Provide reproducible examples when reporting bugs.
- Offer constructive, actionable feedback during reviews.
- Accept review decisions with professionalism.
- Prioritize long-term ecosystem stability over personal preference.
- Admit mistakes promptly and correct them responsibly.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks, hostility, or harassment.
- Dismissive or belittling responses to contributors.
- Trolling, inflammatory language, or unrelated political arguments.
- Publishing private information without explicit consent.
- Disruptive behavior in issues, pull requests, or discussions.
- Repeated low-effort contributions that ignore documented standards.

This is an engineering environment — not a social debate forum.

---

## Technical Conduct Expectations

In addition to behavioral standards, contributors are expected to:

- Follow documented contribution guidelines.
- Respect architectural boundaries.
- Avoid breaking changes without prior discussion (RFC, if applicable).
- Provide tests for compiler, semantic, or runtime modifications.
- Avoid force-pushing to shared branches unless authorized.

Failure to follow contribution processes may result in rejected pull requests or restricted access.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

## Enforcement Framework

Enforcement follows a proportional response model.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Minor unprofessional behavior.

**Action:** Private clarification and guidance.

---

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** Repeated minor issues or moderate violation.

**Action:** Formal written warning. Temporary restrictions may apply.

---

### 3. Temporary Suspension

**Impact:** Serious violation or continued misconduct.

**Action:** Temporary removal of interaction privileges.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** Sustained harassment, aggression, or severe misconduct.

**Action:** Permanent removal from all Veltrix community spaces.

---

## Maintainer Authority

Maintainers reserve the right to:

- Close issues that are off-topic or disruptive.
- Reject contributions that do not meet architectural or quality standards.
- Moderate discussions to maintain a high signal-to-noise ratio.
- Remove contributors who repeatedly violate guidelines.

All moderation decisions aim to protect project integrity and community health.
